The Silent Seconds Murder Case Z-CS-SI-2024-001-Y

About the event

With the rise of algorithms, gamification, and reward systems, our daily lives have been subtly infiltrated, often leading us to unwittingly invest more time and energy. This hidden consumption is largely unnoticed and can be quite harmful.

This installation visualizes and enacts the process by which this hidden consumption accumulates until it ultimately spirals out of control. Due to the one-time and irreversible nature of the installation, once the performance is complete, the piece is rendered inoperative, symbolizing the irreversible breaking of life's balance, thus reinforcing the conceptual message of the work.

In creating the work, I employed two opposing logics: the killer's logic to construct the precise crime scene, and the detective logic in the layout to lead the audience to uncover the truth. This contrast supports the project's content and challenges my own thought process.

On one hand, I aim to analyze how these intricate mechanisms control and deplete human behavior through the work. On the other hand, I also recognize that in the creation process, I am similarly controlling the audience's experience through precise logic. The dualities of control and being controlled, consumption and being consumed, lie at the project's core, aiming to provoke reflection on the behavioral logic of the digital age.

Mystery Serial Case

Recently, we have discovered a series of strikingly related cases across multiple locations, each crime scene exhibiting startlingly similar characteristics. As the lead investigator, I feel as though I have been pulled into a fog.

Taking the crime scene at this specific time as an example

Although these cases present similar scenes, there are still many differences. These characteristics seem to reveal a pattern, but the underlying cause and the intentions of the manipulator remain shrouded in mystery.

Case Observations

With so many mysteries still unresolved, I have decided to begin with the most direct form of evidence collection at the crime scene.

For various reasons, the moment when time stops differs across regions, ranging from approximately 24 to 40 minutes. Perhaps this is related to something within the installation itself.

Observing the liquid part of the installation, a clear and intuitive flow can be seen: the red liquid drips from the pipe, falls onto the fabric, and under certain conditions, the liquid on the fabric spills onto the ground, forming a striking trace.

Below the installation, there is a moving acrylic finger interacting with a phone, performing tapping actions. They seem to signify something.

Crime Scene Analysis

Questions of the Detective

On the surface, the case appears to have no leads, but numerous indications point to this being a silent murder, one that is both hidden and meticulously orchestrated.

Interviews with Victims, Families, and Friends

To understand the hidden connections behind these items, I decided to start by investigating the victims' lives. I interviewed several family members and friends of the victims, trying to find a common clue from their perspectives.

I recorded the information and compiled it into a table after the interviews. (2024.12.10)

Through these interviews, I found that although the victims had completely different identities, they were all, without exception, attracted by certain online platforms and gradually developed a behavior of excessive dependence.

Evidence Organization

To further understand the real life trajectories of these victims, I reviewed their phone records and private diaries. What I discovered was a significant dissonance between their feelings and actual behaviors. This dissonance might just be the key element of the case.

Summary: the victims' perceptions were severely distorted. The platform's design, through precise rewards and content engagement, interfered with their sense of time. This manipulation was not overt, yet powerful enough to make them, unknowingly, victims of time.

Logical Structuring

From the victim's daily life to the platform's manipulation logic, it seems we've found a direction. By using modeling techniques to reconstruct the crime scene, we discovered that this installation not only recreates the entire process of manipulation but also serves as a symbolic reenactment of the relationship between the victim and the perpetrator.

After completing the investigation, we have uncovered key findings. It is now time to move on to the clue board and begin organizing the pieces of the puzzle, piecing together the larger picture.

The Clue Board

Case Choose: The Invisible Killer

It turns out that the murderer we have been chasing all along is the fingertip of each and every one of us.

Ultimately, the culprit I've been pursuing is not a person, but a mechanism—a force that pervades the digital age, invisible yet omnipresent. It is, in part, the victims themselves. Each of us is a potential victim, unknowingly becoming "self-enslavers" of the digital era. Behind these seemingly harmless actions, our freedom is being quietly eroded. The mechanism doesn't just control our behavior; it redefines what freedom means. And that is what is truly alarming.

But such a truth is not something everyone can bear. I have decided to archive this content, marking it as classified file Z-CS-SI-2024-001-Y. It not only records every clue of this case but also reveals the deepest wound of our time. I hope this file will serve as a warning for the future—perhaps one day, we can reclaim the freedom that has been manipulated. But for now, it must remain permanently sealed, waiting for the right moment.

Reflection and Summary as a Creator

Why this topic?

Check-ins, coin rewards, and achievement incentives—these are just a few examples of how algorithms and gamification mechanisms have captures us with an intricate web. These tools, seemingly harmless and entertaining, subtly manipulate our behavior without being noticed.

These mechanisms act as precise "instruments" and, in some ways, have become the "murder" of our lives. By leveraging reward-driven systems and hidden reinforcement mechanisms, they keep our attention locked, consuming our time, energy, and self-control.

This is why I chose to design the installation as a "crime scene." This setting not only symbolizes the invisible violence of behavioral manipulation but also aims to make the audience aware that, in this digital era, each of us could become a victim of this "silent murder."

Why this dual narrative approach?

In this project, I conducted a bold experiment by interpreting the installation from two opposing perspectives: the perpetrator and the detective.

This dual narrative approach added greater depth and complexity to the storytelling, allowing the audience to explore the theme from different angles. In the creating process, I adopted the perspective of the perpetrator to design and construct the installation, while in the layout phase, I shifted to the detective's perspective to guide the audience through uncovering the truth.

1. The Perpetrator's Perspective: Precision and Ruthlessness in Control

During the creation of the installation, I used meticulous calculations and craftsmanship to construct a cold and precise "crime scene." The video showcased the performative process of the installation.

2. The Detective's Perspective: Trace the Evidence

In the layout, I narrated from the perspective of a detective, gradually revealing clues and engaging in logical reasoning to guide the audience toward uncovering the hidden mechanisms of control.

In the research section, I incorporated real discussions, surveys, and interviews. These authentic data were seamlessly woven into the narrative alongside fictional elements, created a "half-true, half-false" storytelling approach, enhanced the immersive quality of the narrative.

Through the dual narrative approach, I aimed to immerse the audience in both the discovery of truth and the experience of manipulation, evoking a sense of tension and contradiction. This alternation between perspectives not only made the work more multidimensional but also lent the storytelling greater authenticity and dramatic intensity.
