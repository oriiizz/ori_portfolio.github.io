Chebei Village Urban Research

2024 | South China University of Technology & The Hong Kong Polytechnic University

About the event

This research project investigates the urban transformation of Chebei Village in Guangzhou through on-site field investigation and participatory observation. Working across architecture, urban studies and human-computer interaction, the team documented spatial narratives, everyday practices and community rhythms that are often overlooked in top-down urban redevelopment discourse.

The fieldwork combined photography, mapping, interviews and environmental documentation to build a grounded understanding of the village's layered histories. Building on these findings, we explored how AI-assisted design tools can support speculative proposals that respect local context rather than erase it—translating empirical research into design scenarios that are both critical and communicative.

The project was developed as a collaborative studio between South China University of Technology and The Hong Kong Polytechnic University in 2024.
