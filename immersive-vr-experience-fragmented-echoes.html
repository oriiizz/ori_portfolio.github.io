Immersive VR Experience: Fragmented Echoes

About the event

In personal growth, a mother serves as both nurturer and guide, and this dual role often unconsciously becomes a form of control. This control is not purely oppressive but arises from a need for love and protection.

The project explores understanding and conflict within the mother-daughter relationship, using a virtual brain space as an interactive medium. It emphasizes the information gap, perceptual differences, and the "limits of understanding" in relationships. Beyond examining the mother-daughter relationship, the project reflects on the idea that rather than seeking absolute understanding in relationships, emotional reconciliation can be achieved through partial understanding and empathy.

Inspiration

The project was inspired by my personal experiences and observations of the mother-daughter relationship throughout my growth. I deeply felt the dual role of my mother as both nurturer and guide. Despite her intentions of love and protection, her excessive care sometimes unconsciously turned into control, causing me to feel conflicted in my pursuit of independence and self-identity. This experience led me to reflect on the delicate balance between control and care within close relationships.

As I grew older, I came to realize that mother's control was not out of malice, but based on deep love and protection. However, even so, communication barriers and cognitive differences between generations can still bring about conflict. This complex emotional experience inspired me to explore the limits of understanding in the mother-daughter relationship and how to bridge the gap through interactive design.
